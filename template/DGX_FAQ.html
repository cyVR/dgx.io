What is the difference between packed bars and cast bars?

Both types of bars come with serial numbers. Both types of bars are also equivalent in gold composition and weight in gold. However, packed bars come with unique designs, and are packaged for display. They contain a higher premium due to the extra workmanship involved in making them. Cast bars, as the name suggests, appear as it is without aesthetic add ons, so have a lower premium compared to packed bars.

Why do you only offer 2 types of gold bars for sale?

Our gold bullion are the lowest priced in the marketplace. We have decided to limit to 2 types of serialised gold bars: 100g Cast Bars and 1000g Cast bars. This was done to keep investorâ€™s premium as low as possible. Any bars sold at a lower denomination with aesthetic add ons contain additional premiums ranging from of 20% to 50% to its intrinsic value, distorting the true price of gold as a commodity. As our main objective is to shield your wealth from a potential economic crisis, we choose to specialise in bars that offer the lowest premiums to the gold investor. As Singapore is also GST and Tax free to purchase investment grade gold, we are able to keep our premiums over spot Gold less than 2%. We continually source for cheaper gold sources so as to bring it as close as we can to the price of gold on the london spot market.
